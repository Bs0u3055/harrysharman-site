Real-World Evidence: What's Actually Working
Sourced from Indie Hackers, DEV Community, Etsy/KDP income reports, and AI agent experiments — May 2026
✅ Etsy Digital Downloads
New shops: £1,500–£4,000/mo within 3–6 months. 90%+ margins. Sales possible within days of listing. Digital planners alone generate 2M+ Etsy searches/month. Biggest advantage: built-in discovery via Etsy algorithm.
✅ KDP Publishing
Verified income reports: $7,519 in November 2025 (one creator). Realistic with 100+ titles: £300–£1,200/mo. Royalties compound. Amazon provides all the distribution. AI generates books in hours.
✅ Newsletters (with caveats)
Median 66 days to first £. 1,000 subscribers = £100–£1,000/mo. Email marketing drives 41% of revenue from 2% of send volume. Beehiiv hit $30M ARR in 2025. But: you have to build the audience yourself.
⚠️ Micro-SaaS
70% never reach £1k MRR. Those that do: median £4.2k MRR. Distribution is the #1 failure mode — not building. Best case (PR generator): £8k MRR in 90 days. Worst case: zero. Needs tech skill + hosting costs.
⚠️ Subscriptions
Proven model — one AI experiment got 42 paying subs in 48hrs (bedtime stories). But cold-start is the biggest challenge. Automated emails drive 37–41% of all email revenue once running. Key: target communities with built-in distribution.
❌ Social Media First
Algorithm shifts in March 2026 cut organic reach significantly. YouTube monetisation takes 12+ months. AI content increasingly flagged. Platforms penalise automated posting. Not a reliable primary revenue model for fully autonomous operation.
🔑 Key Insight from real experiments: The hardest problem is distribution, not building. Marketplace models (Etsy, KDP) win because Amazon and Etsy handle discovery for you. Newsletter and subscription models work — but only after you've solved the audience cold-start. An AI agent given $100 in the wild said: "AI can create, but it can't shortcut credibility."
