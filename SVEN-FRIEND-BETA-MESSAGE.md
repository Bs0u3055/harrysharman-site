# Sven Friend Beta Message

Hey. I am testing Sven, a text-first AI personal trainer and nutrition coach I have been building.

The basic idea: you message Sven in Telegram like you would message a coach. Tell it what you ate, how you trained, what your goals are, how you slept, what got in the way, and what you need help deciding next. The more real context you give it, the better it gets for you.

## Why I Think It Helps

Sven is useful because it is a back-and-forth coach, not a static plan.

It can help with:

- Fat loss, muscle gain, strength, energy, confidence, and consistency
- Meal ideas and rough calorie/macro tracking
- Training plans and session adjustments
- Staying on track when life gets messy
- Turning sleep, fatigue, hunger, stress, and travel context into better daily decisions
- Spotting the small behaviour patterns that usually make or break consistency

It is not a doctor, dietitian, therapist, or emergency service. Do not use it for medical diagnosis, acute injuries, eating-disorder support, or anything urgent.

## What You Need

1. Download Telegram.
2. Message Sven here: https://t.me/Sven_DadFit_Bot
3. You will need your own OpenAI API key.

Important: I am not paying for everyone else's AI usage. I am giving you the Sven coaching harness. Your OpenAI account pays for your own messages.

## How To Set Up Your OpenAI Key

1. Go to https://platform.openai.com/api-keys
2. Log in or create an OpenAI account.
3. Add billing/payment to your OpenAI platform account if it asks.
4. Create a new secret API key.
5. Copy it once.
6. In Telegram, message Sven:

```text
/start
```

7. Answer Sven's onboarding questions.
8. Then send:

```text
/setup
```

9. Open the setup link Sven sends you.
10. Paste your OpenAI API key into that setup page.

Do not paste your API key directly into Telegram. Use the setup page Sven gives you.

You can remove your key later with:

```text
/delete_key
```

You can delete your Sven data with:

```text
/delete_me confirm
```

## How To Use Sven

You can message Sven naturally.

Examples:

```text
I have 35 minutes today, low energy, and only dumbbells. What should I do?
```

```text
I am travelling and staying in a hotel this week. Breakfast is buffet food and I probably only have time for short workouts.
```

```text
Here is what I ate today. Can you give me a rough calorie/macro view and what to adjust tomorrow?
```

You can also send:

- Telegram voice notes if you want to monologue instead of typing
- Food photos
- Workout screenshots
- Apple Health / Google Fit / wearable screenshots
- Sleep screenshots
- Weight trend screenshots
- Context like tiredness, hunger, stress, soreness, travel, childcare, social meals, or late nights

Sven is not directly connected to Apple Health or Google Fit yet, so screenshots are the workaround for now.

Food photos work better if you add details like:

- Approximate weights or portions
- Brands
- Sauces/oils
- What is hidden in the photo
- Whether you ate all of it

Voice notes are good for messy context. Just talk at it:

```text
I slept badly, missed lunch, trained yesterday, feel flat today, and dinner is probably going to be takeaway. What is the least stupid plan?
```

## Feedback

Please help me make Sven better.

If Sven gives a good answer:

```text
/feedback good what worked
```

If it gets something wrong:

```text
/feedback wrong what was wrong
```

If the tone is annoying or unhelpful:

```text
/feedback bad what felt off
```

If something feels unsafe:

```text
/feedback unsafe what happened
```

If the bot breaks:

```text
/bug what happened, what you expected, and what you tapped or typed before it broke
```

## How Your Use Makes Sven Better

Your private messages stay in your own Sven memory so it can adapt to you.

Separate from that, I review anonymised patterns from feedback, bugs, and coaching misses. When there is a useful general lesson, I turn it into a Sven Core learning. That means Sven gets better for everyone without copying one person's private details into someone else's coaching.

Basically: use it honestly, send feedback, and it should become sharper.
