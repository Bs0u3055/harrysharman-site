Let's start with a confession: most of us don't want to think more—we want to think less. We don't crave more meetings, decisions, or to-do lists. We want that appointment booked. That tax return filed. That holiday planned.

Enter: AI agents. They're not just ChatGPT with a fresh haircut. Agents are AI systems that don't just respond—they act. They take your goal and go do it.

And here's the kicker: it's working because we're lazy. Every great technology succeeds not because it's clever, but because it meets humans where we actually are.

The question isn't whether agents work. It's what happens when most cognitive labour gets unbundled and automated. What's left for us?

Turns out, quite a lot. The messy, ambiguous, deeply human stuff. Strategy. Creativity. Empathy. Judgement. The things that require context a machine can approximate but never truly feel.
